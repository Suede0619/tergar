# User Personas for Tergar Retreats Marketing Strategy

Based on the RFP, there are **three primary audiences** your work needs to appeal to, plus **one internal stakeholder** who will evaluate your proposal. These personas are designed to be simple, actionable, and directly tied to the campaign goals.

---

## Persona 1: The Major Donor (Capital Campaign Focus)

### **The Conscious Philanthropist**

**Demographics:**
- Age: 45-65
- Net worth: $5M-$50M+
- Location: Major metro areas (SF, NYC, LA, Boulder, Seattle)
- Background: Tech executive, entrepreneur, or inherited wealth

**Psychographics:**
- Has attended meditation retreats (possibly Tergar, IMS, or Spirit Rock)
- Values: Authenticity, sustainability, long-term impact, legacy
- Seeks meaning beyond material success
- Wants to support something “real” and transformational
- Skeptical of superficial or commercialized spirituality

**Pain Points:**
- Overwhelmed by donation requests from nonprofits
- Wants to see clear vision, competent execution, and measurable impact
- Concerned about mission drift or dilution of teachings
- Needs to justify large gifts to spouse/family/advisors

**What They Need from Your Marketing:**
- **Credibility**: Professional materials that show this is a serious, well-managed project
- **Vision**: Clear articulation of the long-term impact and legacy
- **Authenticity**: Evidence that this honors the lineage and isn’t just another wellness center
- **Transparency**: Honest communication about timeline, budget, and challenges
- **Connection**: Personal relationship with Mingyur Rinpoche or senior leadership

**Key Messaging:**
- “Building a sanctuary for profound practice that will serve generations”
- “Sustainable, authentic, and rooted in an unbroken lineage”
- “Your legacy in service of wisdom and compassion”

**Channels:**
- Personal outreach (email, phone, in-person meetings)
- High-quality donor deck and case for support
- Private donor events (virtual and in-person)
- Website (credibility check)

## Persona 2: The Foundation/Grant Officer (Institutional Funding)

### **The Grant Officer**

**Demographics:**
- Age: 35-55
- Role: Program Officer at foundation (Buddhist-aligned or contemplative science focus)
- Education: Graduate degree (often in nonprofit management, social work, or related field)

**Psychographics:**
- Evaluates dozens of grant proposals per cycle
- Values: Evidence-based impact, organizational capacity, clear metrics
- Personally interested in contemplative practice but professionally rigorous
- Needs to justify recommendations to board/leadership

**Pain Points:**
- Limited time to evaluate each proposal
- Needs to see organizational competence and sustainability
- Concerned about projects that are overly ambitious or poorly planned
- Must demonstrate alignment with foundation’s mission and priorities

**What They Need from Your Marketing:**
- **Clarity**: Clear, concise case for support with specific goals and metrics
- **Professionalism**: Well-organized materials that are easy to review
- **Feasibility**: Realistic timeline and budget with contingency planning
- **Impact**: Clear articulation of who will benefit and how
- **Sustainability**: Evidence of long-term planning and diverse funding sources

**Key Messaging:**
- “Expanding access to authentic contemplative practice”
- “Building organizational capacity for long-term sustainability”
- “Measurable impact on practitioners, local community, and global sangha”

**Channels:**
- Grant proposals and supporting documents
- Website (due diligence research)
- Email updates and reports
- Site visits (if applicable)

**Critical Deadline:**
- **March 2026** - Major grant deadline mentioned in RFP

## Persona 3: The Future Retreatant (Public Phase Focus)

### **The Seeking Millennial/Gen Z**

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28-42 (millennials) or 22-30 (Gen Z)
- Income: $60K-$150K (professional class)
- Location: Urban/suburban, globally distributed
- Background: Tech, creative, healthcare, education sectors

**Psychographics:**
- Spiritually curious but not traditionally religious
- Has tried meditation apps, yoga, maybe a weekend retreat
- Values: Authenticity, experience over possessions, personal growth
- Skeptical of institutions but hungry for genuine wisdom
- Wants community and connection, not just solo practice
- Concerned about climate change and social justice

**Pain Points:**
- Overwhelmed by modern life, seeking deeper meaning
- Skeptical of “spiritual bypassing” or commercialized wellness
- Intimidated by traditional Buddhist settings (language, culture, commitment)
- Financial constraints (needs scholarships or affordable options)
- Time constraints (can’t commit to 3-year retreat, but wants more than a weekend)

**What They Need from Your Marketing:**
- **Accessibility**: Clear explanation of what to expect, no insider jargon
- **Authenticity**: Evidence this is “the real thing,” not watered down
- **Inclusivity**: Welcoming to all backgrounds, not just Buddhists
- **Inspiration**: Beautiful visuals, compelling storytelling, aspirational but achievable
- **Practical Info**: Clear pricing, scholarship info, what’s included, how to prepare

**Key Messaging:**
- “A place to discover who you are beneath the noise”
- “Ancient wisdom, modern approach, welcoming to all”
- “From weekend retreats to year-long immersions—meet yourself where you are”

**Channels:**
- Instagram, TikTok, YouTube (visual storytelling)
- Podcasts (interviews with Mingyur Rinpoche or retreat participants)
- Website (primary research and booking tool)
- Email newsletter (nurture sequence)
- Word-of-mouth from existing Tergar community

**Timeline:**
- **Late 2026** - Public launch phase begins

## Persona 4: The Serious Practitioner (3-Year Retreat Focus)

### **The Committed Student**

**Demographics:**
- Age: 30-60 (wide range)
- Background: Years or decades of meditation practice
- May have completed previous long retreats (1 month, 3 months, etc.)
- Financially stable enough to step away for 3+ years

**Psychographics:**
- Deep commitment to the path and to Mingyur Rinpoche’s lineage
- Values: Authentic transmission, qualified teachers, proper retreat conditions
- Willing to make significant life sacrifices for practice
- Seeks the “real deal”—traditional, rigorous, transformative

**Pain Points:**
- Limited options for long-term retreat in the West
- Concerns about quality of facilities, teachers, and support
- Needs to know the retreat will actually happen (construction timeline)
- May need scholarship support

**What They Need from Your Marketing:**
- **Credibility**: Evidence of authentic lineage and qualified teachers
- **Details**: Specific information about retreat structure, teachers, facilities
- **Timeline**: Realistic construction and launch timeline
- **Scholarship Info**: Financial support options

**Key Messaging:**
- “A traditional 3-year retreat in the West, guided by authentic masters”
- “The opportunity of a lifetime to dedicate yourself fully to the path”

**Channels:**
- Tergar community channels (email, existing sangha)
- Direct outreach to advanced practitioners
- Website (detailed retreat information)

## Internal Stakeholder: The Decision-Maker

### **“Christy Gillham” - Development Manager (RFP Contact)**

**Role:**
- Evaluating proposals and making recommendations
- Will be your primary point of contact if selected
- Needs to justify selection to leadership and board

**What She Needs from Your Proposal:**
- **Confidence**: You understand the mission deeply and can execute
- **Clarity**: Clear scope, timeline, and pricing
- **Partnership**: You’ll be an extension of their team, not just a vendor
- **Value**: Strong ROI on limited budget
- **Alignment**: Your values and approach match Tergar’s ethos

**Evaluation Criteria (from RFP):**
1. Alignment with mission and values
2. Strength of strategic thinking
3. Quality of past work
4. Clarity of process
5. Cost/value alignment
6. Ability to serve as a long-term partner

## How to Use These Personas

### In Your Proposal:

- Show you understand these distinct audiences and their needs
- Demonstrate how your strategy will speak to each effectively
- Explain how your web3/digital expertise reaches Persona 3 (Alex) in ways traditional agencies can’t

### In Your Marketing Strategy:

- **Phase 1 (Quiet)**: Focus on Personas 1 & 2 (donors and grantors)
- **Phase 2 (Public)**: Shift focus to Persona 3 (future retreatants)
- **Throughout**: Maintain communication with Persona 4 (serious practitioners in existing Tergar community)

### In Your Messaging:

- Donors want **legacy and impact**
- Grantors want **evidence and sustainability**
- Retreatants want **authenticity and transformation**
- Practitioners want **tradition and rigor**

Your unique positioning (devotion + digital) allows you to speak authentically to ALL of these audiences in ways that traditional agencies cannot.
